#1

Home Office

Whether you work from home or have carved out a space for something you love like crafting, reading, or writing, your home office is a place that should inspire the flow of creativity and allow you to get down to business.

#2

Curtains, textiles and flowers

Your space should inspire you — and reflect your unique personality.
Add green & breathe some life into your space, so you'll want to stay longer.
Decorate with images that speak to you.
A mix of vintage and modern makes a home feel warm and lived in.

#3

Practice Hygge

“Neutral colors on your walls and floor, paired with natural wood, plants and some kind of textile, like blankets, throw pillows, rugs or a yarn wall hanging, create a simple yet cozy working space.” — Caitlin & Manda, Co-founders of The Merrythought

#4

Let Light in

Stay connected to the great outdoors. Natural light makes a space feel bigger—which is always a plus in a small space. This allows enough light to feed the plants in the room and keep them, as well as myself, lively! And when you feel alive, you’re always more efficient.
